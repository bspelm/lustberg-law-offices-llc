How Shoplifting Is Defined in New Jersey

In New Jersey, shoplifting encompasses a variety of actions beyond merely walking out of a store with unpaid merchandise. State law outlines several behaviors that qualify as shoplifting, including:

  • Taking possession of goods with the intent to deprive the merchant of the full retail value
  • Altering or removing price tags and labels
  • Concealing merchandise on person or within another item
  • Under-ringing items at checkout
  • Transferring merchandise from one container to another to misrepresent its value

The value of the goods involved often determines the severity of the charges. In some instances, what might appear to be a minor mistake can turn into a criminal charge that impacts your future.

Penalties Associated with Shoplifting

New Jersey categorizes shoplifting offenses based on the retail value of the stolen item or items. The range of possible penalties includes community service, fines, probation, and incarceration. Here’s a general breakdown:

  • Items valued under $200: Charged as a disorderly persons offense with up to 6 months in jail and fines up to $1,000
  • $200 to $500: Fourth-degree crime, potentially resulting in up to 18 months in prison
  • $500 to $75,000: Third-degree crime, carrying a sentence of 3 to 5 years in prison
  • Over $75,000: Second-degree crime, which could lead to 5 to 10 years in prison

Depending on past convictions and circumstances of the case, the penalties may be more severe. Having a shoplifting lawyer NJ evaluate your case early on can be the key to preventing the worst outcomes.

A seasoned shoplifting lawyer NJ can analyze these details and present a defense tailored to your situation. They can challenge questionable evidence, negotiate for reduced charges, or even work toward a complete dismissal of your case, depending on the circumstances.
